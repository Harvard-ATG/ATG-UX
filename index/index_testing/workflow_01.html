Usability Testing - Task Worksheet

Part I: Define the objectives of this study

The primary goal of this initial user research is to learn about faculty's workflow when collecting, organizing and sharing academic material with students.
Secondarily, the details of this academic material, whether it is Library material, PDF or Word, Canvas or external links, and more specifically (if applies) images.
This first phase of user meetings are not necessarily focused on Media Manager. The next phase may involve user testing on Dev environment user interfaces, prototypes or screen captures.

Part II: Transform into Task Scenarios

  1. As you organize your course, do you need to share any academic material with students? (the answer may lead to the description of the type of academic material)
  2. How the sharing of above academic material benefits the learning experience of your students?
  3. How do you collect and organize this material and how do you expect your students to access it and to use it?
  4. Do you think that your current process of collecting and organizing academic material could be improved and how?
  5. Do you think that the current process for students to access and use academic material could be improved and how?
  6. (If this topic didn't come up yet)
    • Do you need or would you like to be able to of categorize or gather this material in different groups for assignments, study groups, etc.?
    • Where do you gather the academic material (hard drive? Dropbox? When do you categorize it?
    • Do you ever need to share images with students?
    • Do you have any metadata in each image?
    • How do you expect students to see all the metadata?
The order of the above tasks is such that if time allows only for the top 3 of 4 questions, most of the objectives of this study would be met.
It is not important if users cannot answer all the above questions. Follow up questions such as "If you had to share academic material with students, how would you like to do that?" may still lead to useful findings.
